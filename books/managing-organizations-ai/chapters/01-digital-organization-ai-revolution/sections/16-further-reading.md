## Further Reading

As managers navigate the complexities of digital transformation and artificial intelligence (AI) in organizational contexts, a thorough understanding of existing research is essential. This section provides a curated list of academic references that will deepen your knowledge of how digital technologies and AI are reshaping organizational value creation.

### Recommended Academic References

1. **Brynjolfsson, E., & McAfee, A. (2014).** The Second Machine Age: Work, Progress, and Prosperity in a Time of Brilliant Technologies. *Norton & Company.*
   - An insightful exploration of how digital technologies are transforming industries and economies, focusing on the implications for businesses and management.

2. **Porter, M. E., & Heppelmann, J. E. (2015).** How Smart, Connected Products Are Transforming Companies. *Harvard Business Review.*
   - This article examines the strategic and operational impacts of smart, connected products on company performance and management practices.

3. **Bostrom, N. (2014).** Superintelligence: Paths, Dangers, Strategies. *Oxford University Press.*
   - A thought-provoking analysis of the potential scenarios and challenges posed by advanced AI systems to organizational decision-making.

4. **Davenport, T. H., & Ronanki, R. (2018).** Artificial Intelligence for the Real World. *Harvard Business Review.*
   - Provides a framework for understanding the practical applications of AI in business and how managers can effectively implement AI technologies.

5. **Vial, G. (2019).** Understanding Digital Transformation: A Review and a Research Agenda. *The Journal of Strategic Information Systems.*
   - Offers a comprehensive review of digital transformation literature and outlines key areas for future research, relevant to strategic management.

6. **Iansiti, M., & Lakhani, K. R. (2020).** Competing in the Age of AI: Strategy and Leadership When Algorithms and Networks Run the World. *Harvard Business Review Press.*
   - Discusses how AI-driven processes are redefining competition and what leaders need to do to harness this shift for competitive advantage.

7. **Mikalef, P., & Pateli, A. (2017).** Information Technology-Enabled Dynamic Capabilities and Their Indirect Impact on Competitive Performance: Findings from PLS-SEM and fsQCA. *Journal of Business Research.*
   - Explores the role of IT in developing dynamic capabilities that enhance competitive performance, emphasizing the managerial implications.

8. **Ransbotham, S., Khodabandeh, S., Fehling, R., LaFountain, B., & Kiron, D. (2018).** Artificial Intelligence in Business Gets Real. *MIT Sloan Management Review.*
   - Analyzes survey data to understand how organizations are implementing AI technologies and the real-world challenges and benefits encountered.

9. **Hess, T., Matt, C., Benlian, A., & Wiesböck, F. (2016).** Options for Formulating a Digital Transformation Strategy. *MIS Quarterly Executive.*
   - Discusses strategic options for digital transformation and provides insights into how managers can effectively guide organizational change.

10. **Westerman, G., Bonnet, D., & McAfee, A. (2014).** Leading Digital: Turning Technology into Business Transformation. *Harvard Business Review Press.*
    - Offers a roadmap for managers to lead digital transformations, highlighting case studies from successful digital leaders.

By engaging with these resources, managers can develop a nuanced understanding of the theoretical and practical dimensions of AI and digital technologies in organizations. This knowledge is crucial for making informed decisions that drive innovation and competitive advantage.

:::manager
**Why should a manager care?** Understanding the dynamics of digital and AI transformation equips managers with the strategic insight needed to navigate and leverage technological shifts effectively. This can lead to improved decision-making, enhanced operational efficiency, and sustained organizational growth.
