# The Organizational Resource Framework

Organizations create value by acquiring, combining, and deploying **resources**. Managers coordinate four types of organizational resources:

1. **Customers** — relationships and market access
2. **Operations** — processes and capabilities
3. **People** — knowledge, skills, and culture
4. **Finance** — capital and performance metrics

Digital technologies enhance how managers coordinate each resource type. In Part I of this book, we examine each in turn.

:::callout
**Key Insight:** Information Systems are not about technology. They are about how managers use information to make better decisions about organizational resources.
:::

:::manager
Before investing in any digital capability, managers should ask: *Which resource does this improve, and what organizational change is required to capture the value?*
:::
