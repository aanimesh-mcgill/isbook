## Learning Objectives

After reading this chapter, you will be able to:

:::checklist
- Understand the historical context of various database systems and their evolution, from flat files to AI-native databases.
- Identify the specific analytics problems each database era aimed to solve and the limitations encountered.
- Analyze real-world examples of companies like Netflix, Amazon, and OpenAI, leveraging diverse database technologies to solve modern analytics challenges.
- Evaluate the applicability of traditional and modern database systems in AI-driven analytics scenarios.
- Recognize the trade-offs between consistency and availability in distributed systems and how these trade-offs impact the choice of databases for analytics.
- Integrate AI-driven techniques like RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) and text-to-SQL in designing database systems for analytics.
:::

In this chapter, we trace the evolution of database systems from their inception to the current landscape where AI plays a pivotal role. By understanding this progression, you will gain insights into why certain technologies emerged, how they addressed specific needs, and the new challenges they introduced. This knowledge is crucial for analytics professionals tasked with selecting the most suitable database technology for specific analytics problems in the age of AI.

The journey begins with flat files and moves through hierarchical and network databases, detailing how each system addressed the data management challenges of its time. We then explore the rise of relational databases and why they became the dominant model for decades. The chapter proceeds to discuss the advent of distributed and NoSQL databases, which emerged in response to the limitations of relational systems, especially in handling large-scale, unstructured data.

Real-world examples illustrate these concepts. For instance, Netflix uses a combination of relational and NoSQL databases to manage its vast content library and deliver personalized user experiences. Similarly, Amazon's use of distributed databases enables it to process millions of transactions efficiently.

In the AI era, the rise of vector databases and knowledge graphs introduces new paradigms for handling complex data types and relationships. These systems are designed to support advanced AI applications, providing the flexibility and scalability required to harness AI's full potential.

By the end of this chapter, you will have a comprehensive understanding of the database ecosystem's evolution, equipping you to make informed decisions about which technology fits which problem in your analytics projects.
