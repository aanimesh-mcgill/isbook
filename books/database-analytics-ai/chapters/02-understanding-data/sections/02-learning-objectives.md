## Learning Objectives

After reading this chapter, you will be able to:

:::checklist
- Distinguish between structured, semi-structured, and unstructured data, providing real-world business examples for each.
- Identify which database technologies are best suited for handling different data types and justify the choice based on business needs.
- Explain how structured data, like transactions in SQL databases, play a crucial role in financial analytics, using banks as a case study.
- Analyze the use of semi-structured data, such as JSON logs in MongoDB, for real-time analytics in social media platforms like Twitter/X and Instagram.
- Assess the importance of unstructured data in AI applications, such as computer vision for image processing or NLP pipelines for email categorization.
- Integrate these data types into comprehensive analytics pipelines, considering their impact on AI training data requirements and performance.
:::

By the end of this chapter, you will have a nuanced understanding of how to leverage different data types for solving complex analytics problems. You will gain insights into the trade-offs involved in database selection, the implications of the CAP theorem, and the balance between consistency and availability—essential knowledge for navigating the modern data ecosystem effectively.
