## Learning Objectives

After reading this chapter, you will be able to:

:::checklist
- Understand and articulate the core components of relational modeling, including entities, attributes, and relationships, and how they apply to analytics.
- Identify and define primary keys, foreign keys, and composite keys, explaining their role in maintaining relational integrity and supporting analytical queries.
- Read and sketch Entity-Relationship Diagrams (ERDs), applying these skills to map out business requirements for analytics scenarios in retail and fintech contexts.
- Examine and enforce business rules and constraints within a relational database to ensure data accuracy and reliability for analytics purposes.
- Evaluate the role of relational design in large enterprises, such as banks, and justify its continued use in modern analytics, especially in scenarios involving customer analytics, order management, and fraud detection.
- Translate complex business questions into relationally structured data models, preparing for effective use of SQL in subsequent chapters.
:::

In this section, we will delve into the foundational elements of relational database design, essential for any analytics professional tasked with solving business problems through data. We'll explore how companies like Netflix and Amazon use these principles to structure their data for efficient querying and robust analytics. By the end of this chapter, you will have a clear understanding of how to leverage relational thinking to address real-world challenges in a technology-driven environment.

Through practical examples and case studies, you will gain insights into why relational databases remain a cornerstone for enterprises, despite the rise of NoSQL and other modern data architectures. This foundational knowledge will empower you to choose the right database technology for your specific analytics challenges.
