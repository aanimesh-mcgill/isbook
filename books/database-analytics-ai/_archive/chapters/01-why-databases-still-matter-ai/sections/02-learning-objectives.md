### Section 1.2: Learning Objectives

After reading this chapter, you will be able to:

:::checklist
- Explain why AI, even in its most advanced forms like ChatGPT, fundamentally relies on databases for data storage, retrieval, and management.
- Differentiate between structured and unstructured data types and their respective roles within analytics pipelines, enhancing your ability to select appropriate storage solutions.
- Analyze real-world examples from companies such as Amazon, Netflix, and OpenAI to understand the diverse applications of databases in handling varied workloads.
- Evaluate the evolution of enterprise data systems, from traditional files to modern AI-native architectures, emphasizing the significance of each evolutionary step.
- Apply the CAP theorem to make informed decisions about consistency, availability, and partition tolerance in distributed database systems.
- Assess which database technology, SQL or NoSQL (e.g., MongoDB), best fits specific analytics problems based on data requirements and workload characteristics.
:::

Understanding these objectives will empower you to navigate the complexities of modern data ecosystems, ensuring you can effectively leverage databases to drive analytics in the age of AI. By exploring the strategic use of databases across industries, you'll gain insights into the practical application of these technologies, allowing you to optimize data handling and decision-making processes within your organization.

The chapter will equip you with the knowledge to make informed choices about database technologies and their alignment with your analytics goals. Let's delve into the details of why databases remain crucial in the AI landscape and how they can be your ally in unlocking the potential of data-driven insights.
